Investment Thesis

Rare Downtown Core Infill in a Supply-Constrained Coastal Market

952–958 Howard Avenue represents a genuinely rare assemblage of Downtown Core zoned land in Dunedin — a market that has consistently delivered premium residential pricing, driven by walkability, a nationally recognized coastal lifestyle, and a structural scarcity of new construction opportunities.

The site's DC zoning framework supports a flexible urban residential program. A conceptual 8-unit townhome layout has been developed and received positive initial City response, providing a meaningful de-risking advantage rare in this zoning category. The site is flat, buildable, utility-connected, and positioned within walking distance of Dunedin's Main Street entertainment district, Hammock Park, and the marina waterfront.

For a developer positioned to execute boutique luxury townhome product, the combination of scarcity, walkability, and buyer demand for new construction in this corridor creates a compelling entry.

Dunedin Market Context

Dunedin has consistently outperformed surrounding Pinellas County submarkets for new construction pricing. The combination of walkability, coastal proximity, a beer and culinary tourism economy, and strong year-round residential demand creates a buyer profile that supports boutique luxury product at premium per-unit economics.

The downtown core is intentionally supply-constrained by land scarcity, existing building stock, and the City's planning approach — a structural factor that protects developer margins for well-positioned new product.
